# Gloucester Planning Applications Analysis

What I was looking at

Planning application volumes across England from 1996 to 2025, with a 
focus on how Gloucester compares to the national picture and its South 
West neighbours.

What stands out nationally

Applications peaked in 2003-2004 at around 700,000 per year, fell sharply 
after the 2008 financial crisis, and have been gradually declining since. 
The 2025 figures are partial so the recent drop looks steeper than it is.

Where Gloucester sits

The South West is the third busiest region in England for planning. Within 
it, Gloucester sits outside the top 15 by volume — which makes sense given 
it's a city council rather than a county or unitary authority. South 
Gloucestershire, Cotswold and Stroud all rank higher, pointing to strong 
development pressure across the wider area.

Gloucester's own volumes mirror the national trend — peaking around 5,300 
in 2003, dropping after 2008, and settling at roughly 2,000-2,500 in recent 
years.

The more interesting finding

The gap between applications received and decisions made tells a useful 
story. During the peak years the council was consistently deciding fewer 
applications than it received. That gap has narrowed considerably since 
2010 — a sign of better efficiency at lower volumes, but worth watching 
if development activity picks up again.

What I'd look at next

Volume alone doesn't tell the full story. Layering in processing times and 
decision types would give a much clearer picture of planning service 
performance.
